盐之颂歌

Salt Oratorio

盐漠的夜晚是世界上最安静的地方之一。我们只需要把一块盐敲响，让三十二个人在星空下听见一块石头唱歌。

I · 构想 · Premise

盐漠的夜晚是世界上最安静的地方之一。我们不需要扩音、不需要音响、不需要电线 — 我们只需要把一块盐敲响。让声音在零下六度的干燥空气中自己传播，让三十二个人在星空下听见一块石头唱歌。

The salt desert at night is one of the quietest places on earth. We needed no amplification, no speakers, no cables — only to strike a piece of salt. Let the sound travel through minus-six-degree dry air on its own, and let thirty-two people hear a stone sing under the stars.

II · 制作 · Making

石琴由阿塔卡马当地工匠用三个月时间，从盐矿深处开采的整块岩盐手工切削而成。每块石琴有自己的音高、自己的共振腔、自己的裂纹纹理。三夜之间，演奏者在不同位置击响不同的石琴，从不重复同一组合。第三夜结束后，所有石琴被埋回开采时的坑洞，用盐矿碎石覆盖。

The stone instruments were hand-carved over three months by local Atacama artisans from solid rock salt mined deep underground. Each carries its own pitch, its own resonant chamber, its own fracture pattern. Over three nights, performers struck different stones from different positions, never repeating the same combination. After the third night, every instrument was buried back in the pit it came from, covered with mine rubble.

III · 进入 · Entering

Patrons 在日落前两小时抵达营地，每人获得一条羊毛毯和一壶热水。没有座位，没有舞台 — 大家散坐在盐漠的结晶地面上。日落之后温度骤降，演奏从第一颗星星出现时开始，到最后一颗石琴被击响后结束。全程约两小时。

Patrons arrived at camp two hours before sunset, each given a wool blanket and a thermos of hot water. No seats, no stage — everyone sat scattered on the salt desert's crystalline surface. After sunset the temperature dropped sharply. The performance began when the first star appeared and ended after the last stone was struck. About two hours in total.

IV · 现存 · What Remains

没有录音。没有照片。没有视频。每位 Patrons 离开时获得一小块岩盐碎片 — 是从石琴切削时留下的碎屑，由工匠收集并分装。碎片会在手心慢慢融化，这是它唯一一次发出声音的方式。

No recording. No photograph. No video. Each Patron left with a small chip of rock salt — shavings collected by the artisans during carving. The chip slowly dissolves in the palm; that is the only other time it will ever make a sound.
