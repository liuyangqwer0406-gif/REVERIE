枭时

Owl Hours

I · 构想 · Premise

北海道的原始森林里住着全世界最安静的鸟类之一 — 毛腿渔鸮。它的叫声不是歌唱，而是一种低沉的、几乎听不见的叹息。我们不录制它、不模仿它、不训练它 — 我们只是在它出没的林子里，用人声回应它的叹息，让两种声音在夜空中短暂地相遇。

Hokkaido's primeval forest is home to one of the world's quietest birds — the Blakiston's fish-owl. Its call is not a song but a low, barely audible sigh. We did not record it, imitate it, or train it — we simply stood in the forest where it lives and answered its sigh with human voice, letting two sounds meet briefly in the night air.

II · 制作 · Making

七位演奏者在八夜期间，每夜 23:00 至 04:00 之间进入林子。没有乐谱、没有指挥、没有约定的信号 — 每个人凭自己的耳朵判断毛腿渔鸮是否在叫，然后决定是否回应、用什么音调回应。有些夜里渔鸮不叫，演奏者就沉默地坐到天亮。

Seven performers entered the forest each night over eight nights, between 23:00 and 04:00. No score, no conductor, no agreed signals — each judged by ear whether the fish-owl was calling, then decided whether and how to answer. Some nights the owl did not call, and the performers sat in silence until dawn.

III · 进入 · Entering

Patrons 在 22:30 于林边小屋集合，每人获得一件防寒外套和一支红光手电（白光会惊扰渔鸮）。步行 20 分钟进入林深处后熄灯，仅靠星光和雪地反射辨认方向。可以坐、可以躺、可以走动，但必须保持安静。凌晨 04:00 向导用红光信号集合，步行返回。

Patrons gathered at the forest-edge cabin at 22:30, each given a cold-weather coat and a red-light torch (white light disturbs the owl). Twenty minutes into the forest, all lights off — navigation by starlight and snow reflection only. Sit, lie down, walk, but remain silent. At 04:00 the guide gathered everyone with a red-light signal and walked them back.

IV · 现存 · What Remains

没有录音。没有照片。没有视频。每位 Patrons 获得一页手绘声谱 — 是一位声学研究者在林中实时记录的渔鸮叫声波形图，标注了每次人声回应的时间戳。声谱不能代表那一夜发生了什么，它只是一张纸上的痕迹。

No recording. No photograph, no video. Each Patron received a hand-drawn spectrogram — a waveform of the fish-owl's call recorded in real time by an acoustic researcher in the forest, annotated with timestamps of each human response. The spectrogram cannot represent what happened that night; it is only a trace on paper.
